Customer segmentation helps understand purchasing behavior, logistics expectations, and profitability differences across groups. The DataCo dataset includes the following segment:

## 1. Consumer
Individual buyers purchasing for personal use.  
**Characteristics:**
- Highly price-sensitive; responds strongly to discounts.
- Lower average order quantity compared to B2B segments.
- Expect faster delivery and transparent tracking.
- Often spread across diverse geographic regions.

**Operational Implications:**
- Requires strong last-mile logistics performance.
- Promotional pricing significantly affects sales and volume.

---

## 2. Corporate
Businesses purchasing in moderate-to-large quantities for office or resale use.

**Characteristics:**
- Larger average order size.
- More predictable reorder cycles.
- Medium sensitivity to discounts.
- Higher expectations for service reliability.

**Operational Implications:**
- Delivery reliability (on_time_delivery) is critical.
- Late or inconsistent shipping erodes business trust.
- Often engages in bulk shipping, increasing load efficiency.

---

## 3. Home Office
Small businesses or freelancers operating out of residential locations.

**Characteristics:**
- Small quantity orders but more frequent.
- Moderate to high sensitivity to delays.
- Mix of both consumer-like and corporate-like behavior.

**Operational Implications:**
- Requires flexible shipping options.
- Shipping delay trends often mirror consumer segment,
  but order profitability may be closer to corporate.

---

# How Segments Are Used in Analysis

## A. Performance Metrics
Segments can be compared on:
- **Sales**  
- **Benefit per order / profit**  
- **Order Item Quantity**  
- **On-Time Delivery Rate**  
- **Average Shipping Delay**  
- **Return frequency** (if applicable)

## B. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Insights
Corporate and Home Office customers often have:
- More stable order cycles  
- Higher revenue contribution  
- Lower marketing cost per customer  

Consumers contribute highly to:
- Seasonal volume spikes  
- Discount-driven demand  

## C. Actionable Strategies
- **For Consumer:** optimize delivery experience and discounts.  
- **For Corporate:** improve SLA consistency and bulk shipping incentives.  
- **For Home Office:** emphasize convenience, reliability, and flexible delivery modes.
